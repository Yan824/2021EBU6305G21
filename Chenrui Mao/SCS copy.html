Strategic Counteroffensive Stage

China's strategic counteroffensive in the Anti-Japanese War was carried out under the situation that the world Anti-Fascist War took a fundamental turning point and the Japanese army gradually lost its strategic initiative. In the summer and autumn of 1943, the Eighth Route Army led by the Communist Party of China took the lead in north China behind enemy lines began to turn into counteroffensive operations, opened the Prelude to the Partial counteroffensive of the Japanese battlefield in China. Subsequently, The Chinese Indian army and the Chinese Expeditionary Force also launched counter-attacks in northern Myanmar and western Yunnan. In order to save the fate of defeat, in the spring of 1944, the Japanese army launched "Operation No. 1" on the front battlefield of China, aiming to open up the communication line of the mainland. In the face of the Desperate Japanese attack, the Kuomintang army suffered a series of defeats and even rout. During this period, local counter-offensives in the liberated areas behind enemy lines led by the Communist Party of China developed continuously, liberating large areas of land and population and forcing the Japanese army to shrink to the points and lines in the occupied areas. The partial counteroffensive of China's Anti-Japanese War firmly contained the main force of the Japanese army and provided strong support to the allied attacks on Japan in other battlefields. In early August 1945, the Soviet Union went to war with Japan after the United States dropped atomic bombs on the country. The Chinese Communist party simultaneously launched a fierce overall counter-offensive in the liberated areas and achieved brilliant results. On August 15, Japan accepted the Potsdam Proclamation and announced its unconditional surrender. Japan formally signed its surrender on September 2.
